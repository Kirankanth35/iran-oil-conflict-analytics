# Research Design

## Objective

Measure how U.S.-Iran geopolitical conflict risk is associated with oil prices, U.S. gasoline prices, market volatility, news attention, and business operating risk.

## Three Research Questions

1. How did crude oil and U.S. gasoline prices behave before, during, and after the conflict event window?
2. Does Iran/Hormuz/oil-related news attention help explain short-term oil-price movement?
3. How strongly do oil-price changes pass through to U.S. gasoline prices, and how can this be converted into a business risk index?

## Analytical Techniques

- Event-window comparison
- Descriptive statistics
- Time-series visualization
- Lag correlation
- Robust OLS regression
- Explainable predictive modeling
- Geospatial mapping
- Business risk scoring

## Why this is useful for business analytics

The project translates geopolitical and energy-market data into metrics executives can use for pricing, operations, supply-chain planning, and strategy. It also demonstrates practical analytics skills across data collection, cleaning, modeling, visualization, and communication.
